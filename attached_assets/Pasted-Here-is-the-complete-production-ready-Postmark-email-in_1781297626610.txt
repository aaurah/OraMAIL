3️⃣ DNS Records (MANDATORY)
These must be added to your DNS provider (Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap, Hostinger, etc.)

A. SPF Record
`
Type: TXT
Name: @
Value: v=spf1 include:spf.mtasv.net ~all
`


---

B. DKIM Record
Postmark gives you a DKIM key like:

`
Type: TXT
Name: pm._domainkey
Value: (DKIM key from Postmark)
`


---

C. Return‑Path (Bounce Domain)
`
Type: CNAME
Name: pm-bounces
Value: pm.mtasv.net
`


---

D. DMARC Record
`
Type: TXT
Name: _dmarc
Value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:dmarc@yourdomain.com
`


---

4️⃣ Inbound Email Receiving (MX + Webhook)
To receive emails into your platform (support@, admin@, reply@, etc.)

A. MX Record
`
Type: MX
Name: inbound
Priority: 10
Value: inbound.postmarkapp.com
`


You may also use:

- @ (root domain)
- subdomain (recommended)
- wildcard *.yourdomain.com

---

B. Set Inbound Domain in Postmark
In Postmark → Server → Inbound Stream:

`
Inbound Domain: inbound.yourdomain.com
`


---

C. Inbound Webhook (JSON POST)
Postmark sends parsed email JSON to your backend:

`
InboundHookUrl: https://yourapp.com/api/inbound-email
`


---

5️⃣ Server Tokens (Critical)
You will have:

- Account Token → for managing servers  
- Server Token → for sending email  
- Inbound Hash → for receiving email  


---

6️⃣ Recommended Security Settings

- Enable Open Tracking (optional)  
- Enable Link Tracking (optional)  
- Use HTTP Basic Auth on inbound webhook
